Urban Growth Projections

A MULTIPLE SCENARIO APPROACH BASED ON POTENTIAL GROWTH FROM THE INDUSTRIAL PARK IN CARACOL

Objectives

Model the region focusing on identifying ‘suitability’ areas in which development ‘as a whole’ should and should not occur based on a mechanism that combines ‘attraction’ and ‘restriction’ elements that contribute to the rationale of an individual, family or firm’s location decision.

Key Activities

• Define all the ‘attraction’ and ‘restriction’ data.

• Identify the historical growth from the region since 1986 and project future territorial growth.

• Carry out population projections across different growth scenarios and analyze potential spatial distributions and densities.

• Calculate the historical change of land use.

Deliverables

• Generate over 100 geospatial layers, to be delivered to Haitian counterparts as well as internal IDB stakeholder.

• Provide guidelines and recommendations for future growth for the communes closest to the PIC.

• Offer a summary and complementary analysis of existing planning tools and documents.

Terrier Rouge Trou-du-Nord Limonade Caracol

Caracol

Caracol comprises an urban area of 43ha where 51% is in a flood area and 49% is outside. The land available for future development only represents 8.11ha. Due to its condition it is important to prevent any expansion of the township and it must be consider to apply an urban suitaitable land use model adding 2ha of roads creating a public space realm of open spaces and wooded areas of 13ha doubling the area for institutional and commercial activities, and setting aside 2ha of land for other productive activities, in terms of residential surface the township could only allocate 18 ha.

Limonade

Limonade comprises an urban area of 155ha, 19% is contained in the flood area and 81% is outside. 72ha from the available land can be developed in the future with a sustaintable urbanization model comprised of 31ha for roads, public spaces, natural or naturalized areas; 30ha for residential uses (in which small scale commerce and other complementary uses are included); 7.2ha for institutional and commercial uses and 2.1ha for other, industry-relateduses.

Trou-du-Nord

Trou-du-Nord has 212.91 ha of urban areas 28% is in flood area and 72% is outside leaving 63 ha available for future development which in a sustainable urbanization model for land use would comprise of 45% for roads, public spaces, natural or naturalized areas; 42% for residential uses (in which small scale commerce and other complementary uses are included); 10% for institutional and commercial uses and 3% for other, industry-relateduses.

Terrier Rouge

Terrier Rouge has an urban area of 130ha where 2% is in the flood area and 98% is outside the risk zone. Its empty land for future development represents 53.7% of the total urban area and with a sustaintable urbanization model it could be developed 25ha for roads, public spaces, natural or naturalized areas; 24.2ha for residential uses (in which small scale commerce and other complementary uses are included); 5.3ha for institutional and commercial uses and 1.73ha for other, industry-relateduses.

Scenarios

The ‘smart’ growth scenario results from the instance in which, attractions and restrictions are ‘maximized’. The computational process selects and creates a hierarchy of all areas with the highest degree of attraction from factors that have been classified as such. It discards areas affected or controlled by factors that ought to restrict settlement.
