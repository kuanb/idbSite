Urban Growth Projections

A MULTIPLE SCENARIO APPROACH BASED ON POTENTIAL GROWTH FROM THE INDUSTRIAL PARK IN CARACOL

Objectives

Model the region focusing on identifying ‘suitability’ areas in which development ‘as a whole’ should and should not occur based on a mechanism that combines ‘attraction’ and ‘restriction’ elements that contribute to the rationale of an individual, family or firm’s location decision.

Key Activities

• Define all the ‘attraction’ and ‘restriction’ data.

• Identify the historical growth from the region since 1986 and project future territorial growth.

• Carry out population projections across different growth scenarios and analyze potential spatial distributions and densities.

• Calculate the historical change of land use.

Deliverables

• Generate over 100 geospatial layers, to be delivered to Haitian counterparts as well as internal IDB stakeholder.

• Provide guidelines and recommendations for future growth for the communes closest to the PIC.

• Offer a summary and complementary analysis of existing planning tools and documents.

Population

A multiple scenario approach to population projections. The scenarios present a detailed analysis of the population of the area study in order to understand the dynamics in place and better predict the impact of development projects and investments and future needs.

Scenarios

The ‘smart’ growth scenario results from the instance in which, attractions and restrictions are ‘maximized’. The computational process selects and creates a hierarchy of all areas with the highest degree of attraction from factors that have been classified as such. It discards areas affected or controlled by factors that ought to restrict settlement.
