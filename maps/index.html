About this Research

TThe Inter-American Development Bank’s (IDB) Emerging and Sustainable Cities Initiative (ESCI) goal is to contribute to the improvement of the quality of life in Latin America and Caribbean (LAC) cities in terms of environmental, urban and fiscal sustainability. Haiti’s Northern Corridor stretches some 70 km between the city of Cap Haïtien and the Dominican Republic border city of Ouanaminthe and hosts a population of some 600,000 people along Route Nationale 6 (RN6).

Beyond the jobs directly created by the development of the Caracol Industrial Park (PIC) for which 20,000 to 40,000 jobs are projected, Haiti's Northern Corridor will see a rapid and significant growth in urbanization and demography.

Goals of the Study

This study seeks to address the very complex question of where and how should development occur in the Northern Development Corridor of Haiti, with special emphasis on how human settlement ought to occur in light of the different dy- namics and forces that will shape population growth and migration in the area. But the complexity is not only because of the challenges inherent to the question itself; it is so, also because the challenges posed by a setting of which there is limited information, whose population dynamics express a volatility strongly in- fluenced by poverty at the rural and urban levels.

In December 2012, the Government of Haiti issued the Cap Haïtien – Ouana- minthe Development Corridor Regional Comprehensive Plan, supported jointly by the USAID and the IDBi. ERM’s goal is to draw from the significant work and find- ings as well as to contribute further on some of the areas and topics covered by this effort. For the purposes of this study, ERM will refer to this as the AIA – RCP.
