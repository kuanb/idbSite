About ESCI @ IDB: The Emerging and Sustainable Cities Initiative (ESCI) is a technical assistance program of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) that helps intermediate cities in Latin America and the Caribbean in identifying prioritizing and structuring projects to improve their environmental, urban and fiscal sustainability. Learn more here.

Background

In 2013, the IDB’s Emerging and Sustainable Cities Initiative (ESCI) started to develop interdisciplinary urban development plans for selected communes in Haiti’s Northern Development Corridor of Haiti.

Area

A 70 km corridor in Northern Haiti, extending along Route Nationale 6 and connecting roughly 600,000 people between Cap Haitian and neighboring Dominican Republic.

Sites

The Corridor contains the Caracol Industrial Park (PIC), which potentially will bring 20,000-40,000 new manufacturing jobs to the region within the next years and will produce a rapid demographic and urban growth in the municipalities of its area of influence.

Goals

  • • Mitigate short-term impacts while ensuring future sustainable growth
  • • Generate and analyze previously unavailable data and information
  • • Achieve the highest level of specificity in both diagnostic and future planning work
  • • Translate a regional vision into site-specific and interdisciplinary plans for the communes closest to the PIC
